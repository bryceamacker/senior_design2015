Robot Design

Overview

The design of the final robot for SECON 2015 was the result of many failed attempts at designing a robot that satisfied all of the competition's requirements. In December the team finished their prototype and named in "Clyde." This prototype could play all the games and do simple navigation but it did not satisfy the size constraint nor could it navigate the entire course. In January the team redesigned the robot to fit the size constraint and use more unified materials such as plexiglass and 3D printed material. This robot earned the name "Bonnie." This page contains all of the 3D models, laser-cut designs, and software that is Bonnie. The vast majority of Bonnie can be recreated by downloading these files and using a laser cutter and 3D printer. Other than these files, a box of screws and various electronics are needed to recreate Bonnie.

3D Parts

The 3D parts for Bonnie were designed in AutoCAD and printed with a Stratys uPrint 3D printer.

Software

Bonnie uses two PIC micro-controllers from Microchip, one for navigation and one for game playing. Specifically the PIC24HJ128GP506A.
