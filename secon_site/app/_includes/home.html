Competition results

MSU placed 17th out of 39 schools at the competition. During the first round Bonnie accumulated 320 points, the second round 110 points, and the final round 110 points. Each round was out of 1000 points. This performance was far inferior to Bonnie's true potential. Before leaving Starkville we could complete three different courses each with a time of three minutes or less. The night before departing, both of our DC motors malfunctioned and had to be replaced, and one sensor array had to be replaced. This caused many issues. During the first round Bonnie successfully navigated to each game and completed the course. She played Simon perfectly and picked up the card as instructed, but was misaligned moving into the Etch-a-Sketch and the Rubiks cube, so these games were not played. During the second round Bonnie knocked the Simon out of the box and got stuck waiting for the first light on the Simon to light up. We had to stop this round early since she would not move out of the first game box. During the final round Bonnie lost the line on the course and got very lost, and we had to stop the round early once again. All-in-all the robot we built was not the robot that we presented at the competition.

Competition Overview

The theme of SECON 2015 is road tripping. Competitors must design a robot that can navigate a course and play four commonly found games on road trips. The robot starts upon a start light, and when the light turns of the round begins. The robot must follow a white line to four different boxes and in each box there is a game. The robot must play Simon for fifteen seconds correctly, draw "IEEE" on an Etch-a-Sketch, twist one row of a Rubiks Cube 180°, and pick up exactly one card from a deck of cards. After all of the games have been completed the robot must then cross a finish line. These tasks must be completed in under five minutes and they must be completed autonomously.
